This document summarizes the analytical approximation calibrated from
\texttt{data.csv}, where the numerical content behaves as
\[
E \; [\mathrm{eV}], \qquad
F(E) \; [(\mathrm{m}^2\,\mathrm{sr}\,\mathrm{s}\,\mathrm{GeV})^{-1}] .
\]

For a flat horizontal detector of area
\(\SI{1}{km^2}\), exposed to an isotropic downward flux, the threshold rate is
\[
R(>E_{\mathrm{th}})
=
\pi \times 10^{-3}
\int_{E_{\mathrm{th}}}^{\infty} F(E)\, dE ,
\]
where \(E\) is measured in eV. The factor \(10^{-3}\) comes from the
combination of
\[
dE_{\mathrm{GeV}} = 10^{-9}\, dE_{\mathrm{eV}}
\qquad \text{and} \qquad
\SI{1}{km^2} = 10^6\,\si{m^2}.
\]

The following piecewise power-law approximation gives
\[
R(>E) \quad [\mathrm{s}^{-1}\,\mathrm{km}^{-2}]
\]
for
\[
10^{10}\,\mathrm{eV} \le E \le 10^{20}\,\mathrm{eV} :
\]

\[
R(>E) \approx
\begin{cases}
4.061 \times 10^8
\left(\dfrac{E}{10^{10}\,\mathrm{eV}}\right)^{-1.5452},
& 10^{10} \le E < 10^{12}\,\mathrm{eV},
\\[1.2em]
3.298 \times 10^5
\left(\dfrac{E}{10^{12}\,\mathrm{eV}}\right)^{-1.6679},
& 10^{12} \le E < 2 \times 10^{15}\,\mathrm{eV},
\\[1.2em]
1.029
\left(\dfrac{E}{2 \times 10^{15}\,\mathrm{eV}}\right)^{-1.9606},
& 2 \times 10^{15} \le E < 5 \times 10^{16}\,\mathrm{eV},
\\[1.2em]
1.870 \times 10^{-3}
\left(\dfrac{E}{5 \times 10^{16}\,\mathrm{eV}}\right)^{-2.2102},
& 5 \times 10^{16} \le E < 3 \times 10^{18}\,\mathrm{eV},
\\[1.2em]
2.196 \times 10^{-7}
\left(\dfrac{E}{3 \times 10^{18}\,\mathrm{eV}}\right)^{-1.9198},
& 3 \times 10^{18} \le E < 3 \times 10^{19}\,\mathrm{eV},
\\[1.2em]
2.642 \times 10^{-9}
\left(\dfrac{E}{3 \times 10^{19}\,\mathrm{eV}}\right)^{-2.6838},
& 3 \times 10^{19} \le E \le 10^{20}\,\mathrm{eV}.
\end{cases}
\]

The approximation was checked against the more exact log-log interpolation and
integration implemented in \texttt{cosmic\_ray\_rate.py}. Over the range
\(10^{10}\) to \(10^{20}\,\mathrm{eV}\), the relative error is approximately
\[
\text{median} \approx 5.45\%, \qquad
\text{95th percentile} \approx 15.62\%, \qquad
\text{worst case} \approx 18.44\%.
\]

For a detector with area \(A\) in \(\si{km^2}\), multiply the above result by
\(A\).
